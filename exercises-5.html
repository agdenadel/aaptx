Section4.3Exercises

1

True/False. For each of the following, write T if the statement is true; otherwise, write F. You do NOT need to provide explanations or show work for this problem. Throughout, let \(G\) and \(G'\) be groups.

  1. Every group contains at least two distinct subgroups.

  2. If \(H\) is a proper subgroup of group \(G\) and \(G\) is finite, then we must have \(|H|\lt |G|\text{.}\)

  3. \(7\Z\) is a subgroup of \(14\Z\text{.}\)

  4. A group \(G\) may have two distinct proper subgroups which are isomorphic (to one another).

Solution

  1. F

  2. T

  3. F

  4. T
2

Give specific, precise examples of the following groups \(G\) with subgroups \(H\text{:}\)

  1. A group \(G\) with a proper subgroup \(H\) of \(G\) such that \(|H|=|G|\text{.}\)

  2. A group \(G\) of order 12 containing a subgroup \(H\) with \(|H|=3\text{.}\)

  3. A nonabelian group \(G\) containing a nontrivial abelian subgroup \(H\text{.}\)

  4. A finite subgroup \(H\) of an infinite group \(G\text{.}\)

Solution

(Other answers are possible.)

  1. \(G=\Z\text{,}\) \(H=2\Z\)

  2. \(G=\Z_{12}\text{,}\) \(H=\{0,4,8\}\)

  3. \(G=GL(2,\R)\text{,}\) \(H=\{\pm I_2\}\)

  4. \(G=\R^*, H=\{\pm 1\}\text{.}\)
3

Let \(n\in \Z^+\text{.}\)

  1. Prove that \(n\Z \leq \Z\text{.}\)

  2. Prove that the set \(H=\{A\in \M_n(\R)\,:\,\det A=\pm 1\}\) is a subgroup of \(GL(n,\R)\text{.}\)

(Note: Your proofs do not need to be long to be correct!)

Solution

  1. We know that \(n\Z\subseteq \Z\) and that \(n\Z\) is a group under addition, the group operation in \(\Z\text{.}\) Therefore, \(n\Z\leq \Z\text{.}\)

  2. Clearly, \(H\) is a nonempty subset of \(GL(n,\R)\) (for instance, \(I_n\in H\)). Next, let \(A,B\in H\text{.}\) Then

    \begin{equation*} \det(AB^{-1})=(\det A)(\det (B^{-1}))=(\pm 1)\left(\frac{1}{\pm 1}\right)=\pm 1, \end{equation*}

    so \(AB^{-1}\in H\text{.}\) Thus, \(H\leq GL(n,\R)\) by the Two-Step Subgroup Test.
4

For each group \(G\) and subset \(H\text{,}\) decide whether or not \(H\) is a subgroup of \(G\text{.}\) In the cases in which \(H\) is not a subgroup of \(G\text{,}\) provide a proof. (Note. Your proofs do not need to be long to be correct!)

  1. \(G=\Z\text{,}\) \(H=\R\)

  2. \(G=\Z_{15}\text{,}\) \(H=\{0,5,10\}\)

  3. \(G=\Z_{15}\text{,}\) \(H=\{0,4,8,12\}\)

  4. \(G=\C\text{,}\) \(H=\R^*\)

  5. \(G=\C^*\text{,}\) \(H=\{1,i,-1,-i\}\)

  6. \(G=\M_n(\R)\text{,}\) \(H=GL(n,\R)\)

  7. \(G=GL(n,\R)\text{,}\) \(H=\{A\in \M_n(\R)\,:\,\det A = -1\}\)

Solution

  1. \(H\not\subseteq G\text{,}\) so \(H\not\leq G\text{.}\)

  2. \(H\leq G\text{.}\)

  3. \(H\) is a subset of \(G\) but isn't closed under \(+_{15}\text{:}\) for instance \(12+_{15}4=1\not\in H\text{.}\) So \(H\not\leq G\text{.}\)

  4. \(H\) is a subset of \(G\text{,}\) but the identity element, 0, of \(G\) isn't in \(H\text{,}\) so \(H\not\leq G\text{.}\)

  5. \(H\leq G\text{.}\)

  6. \(H\) is a subset of \(G\text{,}\) but the identity element, \(\0\text{,}\) of \(G\) isn't in \(H\text{,}\) so \(H\not\leq G\text{.}\)

  7. \(H\) is a subset of \(G\text{,}\) but the identity element, \(I_n\text{,}\) of \(G\) isn't in \(H\text{,}\) since \(\det I_n=1\text{.}\) Thus, \(H\not\leq G\text{.}\)
5

Let \(G\) and \(G'\) be groups, let \(\phi\) a homomorphism from \(G\) to \(G'\text{,}\) and let \(H\) a subgroup of \(G\text{.}\) Then \(\phi(H)\) is a subgroup of \(G'\text{.}\)

Solution

Let \(x,y\in \phi(H)\text{.}\) Then \(x=\phi(a)\) and \(y=\phi(b)\) for some \(a,b\in H\text{.}\) So \(xy=\phi(a)\phi(b)=\phi(ab)\) (since \(\phi\) is a homomorphism). Since \(H\leq G\text{,}\) \(ab\in H\text{.}\) Thus, \(xy\in \phi(H)\text{.}\) Next, \(e_G\in H\text{,}\) so \(e_{G'}=\phi(e_G)\in \phi(H)\text{.}\) Finally, since \(H\leq G\) and \(a\in H\text{,}\) \(a^{-1}\in H\text{;}\) so \(x^{-1}=\phi(a)^{-1}=\phi(a^{-1}) \in \phi(H)\text{.}\) Thus, \(\phi(H)\leq G'\text{.}\)
6

Recall that an element \(u\) of a binary structure is said to be an idempotent if \(u^2=u\text{.}\) Let \(G\) be an abelian group, and let \(U\) be the set of all idempotents of \(G\text{.}\) Prove that \(U\) is a subgroup of \(G\text{.}\)

Solution

Clearly, \(e\in H\text{,}\) so \(H\neq \emptyset\text{.}\) Next, let \(u,v\in H\text{.}\) Then

\begin{align*} (uv^{-1})^2\amp =(uv^{-1})(uv^{-1})\amp \amp\\ \amp =u^2(v^{-1})^2 \amp \amp \text{ (since the group operation is assoc. and comm.) }\\ \amp =u^2(v^2)^{-1} \amp \amp \text{ (using the rules for exponents) }\\ \amp =uv^{-1}\amp \amp \text{ (since \(u,v\in H\)). } \end{align*}

Thus, \(uv^{-1}\in H\text{,}\) and so \(H \leq G\) by the Two-Step Subgroup Test.

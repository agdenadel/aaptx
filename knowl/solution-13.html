Solution2.8.2.1

Let \(f,g\in G\text{.}\) Then for every \(x\in \Z\text{,}\) \begin{equation*} (fg)(x)=f(x)g(x)=g(x)f(x), \end{equation*} since \(f(x), g(x)\in \R\) and multiplication of real numbers is commutative. Since \(g(x)f(x)=(gf)(x)\text{,}\) \(fg=gf\text{,}\) and so pointwise multiplication on \(G\) is commutative.

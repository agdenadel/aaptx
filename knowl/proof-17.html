Proof

Define \(\phi:\R\to \R^+\) by \(\phi(x)=e^x\text{.}\) Our map \(\phi\) is a homomorphism since for every \(x,y\in \R\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} \phi(x+y)=e^{x+y}=e^xe^y=\phi(x)\phi(y). \end{equation*}

Moreover, \(\phi\) is a bijection, since it has inverse function \(\ln x: \R^+\to \R\text{.}\) Hence, \(\R \simeq \R^+\) via isomorphism \(\phi\text{.}\)

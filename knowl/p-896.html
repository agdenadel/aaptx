The number of partitions of a finite set of \(n\) elements gets large very quickly as \(n\) goes to infinity. Indeed, there are 52 partitions of a set containing just 5 elements! 1 But our goal here is not to count the number of partitions of a given set, but rather to use particular partitions of a group \(G\) to help us study that group's structure.

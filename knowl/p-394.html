We say that two groups \(G\) and \(G'\) (or binary structures \(S\) and \(S'\)) are isomorphic, and write \(G\simeq G'\text{,}\) if there exists an isomorphism from \(G\) to \(G'\text{.}\) We say that \(G\) is isomorphic to \(G'\) via \(\phi\) if \(\phi\) is an isomorphism from \(G\) to \(G'\text{.}\) If there exists no isomorphism from \(G\) and \(G'\text{,}\) then we say that \(G\) and \(G'\) are nonisomorphic, and write \(G\not\simeq G'\text{.}\)

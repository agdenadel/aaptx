The homomorphism \(c_a\) is called conjugation by \(a\). Homomorphisms from a group \(G\) to itself are called automorphisms. Thus, conjugation by any element \(a\) in \(G\) is an automorphism of \(G\text{.}\) (Beware: Some texts use “conjugation by \(a\)” to refer to the function \(x\mapsto a^{-1}xa\text{.}\)) Both versions of conjugation by \(a\) in group \(G\) are automorphisms of \(G\text{.}\))

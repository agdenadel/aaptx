Define \(\Phi: \R^* \rightarrow \R^+\) by \(\Phi(x)=|x|\text{.}\). We know that \(\Phi\) is a homomorphism, since \(\Phi(xy)=|xy|=|x||y|=\Phi(x)\Phi(y)\text{,}\), for every \(x,y\in \R^*\text{.}\). Moreover, \(\Phi\) is clearly onto (so \(\Phi(\R^*)=\R^+\)), and has \begin{equation*} \Ker \Phi=\{x\in \R^*\,:\,\Phi(x)=1\}=\{1,-1\}=N. \end{equation*}

Finally, let \(A\in G\text{.}\) Since \(\det A\neq 0\text{,}\) \(A\) has (matrix multiplicative) inverse \(A^{-1}\) in \(\M_2(\R)\text{.}\) But we need to verify that \(A^{-1}\) is in \(G\text{.}\) This is in fact the case, however, since \(A^{-1}\) is invertible (it has inverse \(A\)), hence \(\det A^{-1} \neq 0\text{.}\) Thus, \(A^{-1}\) is also in \(G\text{.}\)

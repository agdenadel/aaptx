Well, let \(x\in aH\text{.}\). Then \begin{equation*} (xH)(a^{-1}H)=xa^{-1}H \end{equation*} and, since \(xH=aH\text{,}\), \begin{equation*} (xH)(a^{-1}H)=(aH)(a^{-1}H)=aa^{-1}H=H. \end{equation*} Since our operation on \(G/H\) is well defined, this means that \(xa^{-1}H=H\text{,}\), so \(xa^{-1}\in H\text{;}\); thus, \(x\in Ha\text{.}\). We conclude that \(aH\subseteq Ha\text{.}\). The proof that \(Ha\subseteq aH\) is similar. So \(aH=Ha\text{,}\), and thus, since \(a\in G\) was arbitrary, \(H\) is normal in \(G\text{.}\).

Do not confuse subgroups with subsets! All subgroups of a group \(G\) are, by definition, subsets of \(G\text{,}\) but not all subsets of \(G\) are subgroups of \(G\) (see Example 3, Parts 2–5, below). Whether or not a subset of \(G\) is a subgroup of \(G\) depends on the operation of \(G\text{.}\)

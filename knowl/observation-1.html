Recall1.1.1
  • \(\forall\) means “for all” or “for every”
  • \(\exists\) means “there exists”
  • \(\therefore\) means “therefore”
  • “WLOG” or “wolog” means “without loss of generality” (we later discuss this in greater detail)
  • \(\Leftarrow\) means “only if”; also also indicates the “only if” direction in proofs
  • \(\Rightarrow\) means “implies”; it also indicates the “if” direction in proofs
  • \(\Leftrightarrow\) or iff means “if and only if”

Finally, the notation \(:=\) means we are assigning a definition. E.g., writing “Let \(S:=\{1,2,3\}\)” means we are defining \(S\) to be the set \(\{1,2,3\}\text{.}\) We often omit the colon and simply write \(=\) in these cases, but we may include it to emphasize the fact that we are defining something.

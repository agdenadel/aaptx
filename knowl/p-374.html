Let \(G\) be a group. The subset \begin{equation*} Z(G):=\{z \in G\,:\, zg=gz \mbox{ for all } g\in Z(G)\} \end{equation*} of \(G\) is called the center of \(G\text{.}\). In other words, \(Z(G)\) is the set of all elements of \(G\) that commute with every element of \(G\text{.}\). Prove that \(Z(G)\) is closed in \(G\text{.}\).

Theorem3.3.3

Let \(\langle G,\cdot\rangle\text{,}\) \(\langle G',\cdot'\rangle\text{,}\) and \(\langle G'',\cdot''\rangle\) be groups.

  1. Group \(G\) is isomorphic to itself.

  2. If \(\phi\) is an isomorphism from \(G\) to group \(G'\text{,}\) then there exists an isomorphism from \(G'\) to \(G\text{.}\) Hence, \(G\simeq G'\) if and only if \(G'\simeq G\text{.}\)

  3. If \(G\simeq G'\) and \(G'\simeq G''\text{,}\) then \(G\simeq G''\text{.}\)

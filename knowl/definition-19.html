Definition2.4.3

For \(n\in \Z^+\text{,}\) we define \(GL(n,\R)\) by

\begin{equation*} GL(n,\R)=\{M\in \M_n(\R):\det M \neq 0\}. \end{equation*}

In other words, \(G\) is the set of all invertible \(n\times n\) matrices over \(\R\text{.}\)

We define subset \(SL(n,\R)\) by \(GL(n,\R)\) to be the set

\begin{equation*} SL(n,\R)=\{M\in \M_n(\R):\det M =1\}. \end{equation*}

We denote a zero matrix by \(\0\) and the \(n\times n\) identity matrix by \(I_n\text{.}\)

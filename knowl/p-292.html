Now, let \(a,b,c\in \Z_n\text{.}\). We want to show that \((a+_n b)+_n c = a +_n(b+_n c)\text{.}\). Now, \begin{align*} (a+_n b)+_n c\amp =R_n(a+b)+_n c\\ \amp \equiv_n R_n(a+b)+c\\\ \amp \equiv_n (a+b)+c\\\ \amp \equiv_n a+(b+c)\\ \amp \equiv_n a+R_n(b+c)\\ \amp \equiv_n a+_n (b+_n c). \end{align*} So \((a+_n b)+_n c\) and \(a+_n (b+_n c)\) are congruent mod \(n\text{.}\). Since both of these values are in \(\{0,1,\ldots, n-1\}\text{,}\), this implies that they are equal, as desired.

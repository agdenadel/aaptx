Solution2.2.4.1

  1. Let \(x, y\in 2\Z\text{.}\) Then there exist \(a,b\in \Z\) such that \(x=2a\) and \(y=2b\text{.}\) Then \begin{equation*} x+y=2a+2b=2(a+b)\in 2\Z. \end{equation*} So \(2\Z\) is closed under addition.

  2. Since, for instance, \(2,3\in S\) but \(2(3)=6\not\in S\text{,}\) \(S\) isn't closed under multiplication.

  3. Let \(A=\begin{bmatrix}a \amp b \\ 0 \amp c\end{bmatrix}\) and \(B=\begin{bmatrix}\alpha \amp \beta \\ 0 \amp \gamma\end{bmatrix}\) in \(U\text{.}\) Then \begin{equation*} AB= \begin{bmatrix} a \amp b \\ 0 \amp c \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \alpha \amp \beta \\ 0 \amp \gamma \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} a\alpha \amp a\beta+b\gamma \\ 0 \amp c\gamma \end{bmatrix} \in U. \end{equation*} So \(U\) is closed under matrix multiplication.

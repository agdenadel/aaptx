Let \(n\) be an integer greater than or equal to \(3\text{.}\). Then, again using the convention that \(f^0=r^0=e\text{,}\), \(D_n\) can be uniquely described as \begin{equation*} D_n=\{f^ir^j: i=0,1, j=0,1,\ldots, n-1\} \end{equation*} with the relations \begin{equation*} rf=fr^{n-1} \text{ and } f^2=r^n=e. \end{equation*}

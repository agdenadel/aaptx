The use of a colon to denote “such that” is only valid in the above set-builder notation context. Outside of this context, you should never use a colon to denote “such that”; instead, write out the actual words or use the abbreviation s.t. (or the symbol \(\ni\text{,}\) though I do not recommended). Conversely, never use one of those ways of indicating “such that” within set-builder notation; always use a colon there. Why? Convention.

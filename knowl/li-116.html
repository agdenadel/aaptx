Item2.8.3.b

  • Matrix multiplication is always associative, and the zero matrix in \(\M_2(\R)\) acts as an additive identity element. Finally, let \(A\in \M_2(\R)\text{.}\) Then \(-A\in \M_2(\R)\) is an inverse for \(A\) under addition. Thus, \(\M_2(\R)\) is a group under addition.

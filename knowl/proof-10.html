Proof

Let \(a,b,c\in G\) and assume that \(ab=ac\text{.}\) Multiplying both equation sides on the left by \(a^{-1}\text{,}\) we obtain \begin{align*} {2} \amp \amp a^{-1}(ab)\amp =a^{-1}(ac)\\ \amp \Rightarrow \amp (a^{-1}a)b\amp =(a^{-1}a)c\\ \amp \Rightarrow \amp eb\amp =ec\\ \amp \Rightarrow \amp b\amp =c \end{align*}

This proves that the left cancellation law holds. A similar proof shows that the right cancellation law holds.

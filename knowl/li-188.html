Item3

  • Let \(I\) be the subset \begin{equation*} I=\R-\Q=\{x\in \R: x \text{ is irrational} \} \end{equation*} of the group \(\R\text{.}\) The set \(I\) is not a group under \(\R\)'s operation \(+\) since it is not closed under addition: for instance, \(\pi, -\pi \in I\text{,}\) but \(\pi+(-\pi)=0\not\in I\text{.}\) So \(I\) is a subset but not a subgroup of \(\R\text{.}\)

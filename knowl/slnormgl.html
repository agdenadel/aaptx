Example8.2.9

Let \(n\in \Z^+\text{.}\) Here is a rather elegant proof of the fact that \(SL(n,\R)\) is a normal subgroup of \(GL(n,\R)\text{:}\) Define \(\phi: GL(n,\R) \to \R^*\) by \(\phi(A)=\det A\text{.}\) Clearly, \(\phi\) is a homomorphism, and since the identity element of \(\R^*\) is 1, \begin{equation*} \Ker \phi=\{A\in GL(n,\R)\,:\,\det A= 1\}=SL(n,\R). \end{equation*}

So \(SL(n,\R)\unlhd GL(n,\R)\text{.}\)

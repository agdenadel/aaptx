Notice that the complete set \(\{E,O\}\) of distinct equivalence classes of \(\Z\) under \(\equiv_n\) is a partition of \(\Z\text{,}\), and the complete set \(\{[\emptyset],[\{a\}],[\{a,b\}],[A]\}\) of distinct equivalence classes of \(P(A)\) under \(\sim\) is a partition of \(P(A)\text{.}\). This is not a coincidence! It turns out that equivalence relations and partitions go hand in hand.

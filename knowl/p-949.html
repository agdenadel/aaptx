Let \(A=\{a,b,c\}\text{,}\), and define \(\sim\) on the power set \(P(A)\) of \(A\) by \(X\sim Y\) if and only if \(|X|=|Y|\text{.}\). It is straightforward to show that \(\sim\) is an equivalence relation on \(P(A)\text{,}\), under which \(P(A)\) has exactly 4 distinct equivalence classes: \begin{equation*} [\emptyset]=\{\emptyset\}, \end{equation*} \begin{equation*} [\{a\}]=[\{b\}]=[\{c\}]=\{\{a\},\{b\}, \{c\}\}, \end{equation*} \begin{equation*} [\{a,b\}]=[\{a,c\}]=[\{b,c\}]=\{\{a,b\},\{a,c\},\{b,c\}\}, \text{ and } \end{equation*} \begin{equation*} [A]=\{A\}. \end{equation*}

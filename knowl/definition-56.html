Definition7.1.9

Given an equivalence relation \(\sim\) on a set \(S\text{,}\) for each \(x\in S\) we define the equivalence class of \(x\) under \(\sim\) to be \begin{equation*} [x]=\{y\in S\,:\, y\sim x\}. \end{equation*}

These sets \([x]\) (\(x\in S\)) are called the equivalence classes of \(S\) under \(\sim\).

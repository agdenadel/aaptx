Let \(F\) be the set of all functions from \(\R\) to \(\R\text{,}\), and define pointwise addition \(+\) on \(F\) by \begin{equation*} (f+g)(x)=f(x)+g(x) \end{equation*} for all \(f,g\in F\) and \(x\in \R\text{.}\). We claim that \(F\) is a group under pointwise addition. (For variety, in this proof we don't explicitly refer to \(\G_1\)â€“\(\G_3\text{,}\), though we certainly do verify they hold.) Indeed, if \(f,g\in F\) then clearly \(f+g\) is also a function from \(\R\) to \(\R\text{,}\), so \(F\) is closed under \(+\text{.}\).

Proof

For Part 1, note that \begin{align*} \phi(e)\cdot'e'\amp =\phi(e)\amp \amp \text{ (by definition of \(e'\)) }\\ \amp =\phi(e\cdot e) \amp \amp \text{ (by definition of \(e\)) }\\ \amp =\phi(e)\cdot'\phi(e) \amp \amp \text{ (since \(\phi\) is a homomorphism). } \end{align*}

Thus, by left cancellation, \(e'=\phi(e)\text{.}\) The proof of Part 2 is left as an exercise for the reader.

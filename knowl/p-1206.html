Next, \(S^1\) is clearly closed under the operation, and associativity follows from associativity of addition in \(\R\text{.}\) Moreover, \(e^{i0}=1\) clearly acts as an identity element in \(S^1\text{,}\) and if \(e^{i\theta}\in S^1\text{,}\) then \(e^{i\theta}\) has inverse \(e^{i(-\theta)} \in S^1\text{.}\) So \(S^1\) is a group under the described operation.

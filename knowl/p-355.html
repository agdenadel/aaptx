Next, since \(\det I_n=1\text{,}\), \(I_n\) is in \(SL(n,\R)\text{,}\), and clearly acts as an identity element in \(SL(n,\R)\text{.}\). Finally, let \(A\in SL(n,\R)\text{.}\). Since \(\det A=1\neq 0\text{,}\), \(A\) has an inverse matrix \(A^{-1}\) in \(GL(n, \R)\text{.}\). \(A^{-1}\) is in \(SL(n,\R)\) since \begin{equation*} \det(A^{-1})=\frac{1}{\det A}=1/1=1. \end{equation*} So \(A\) has an inverse in \(SL(n,\R)\text{.}\).

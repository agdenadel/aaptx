If \(x=a^{-1}b\) and \(y=ba^{-1}\text{,}\), then \(ax=a(a^{-1}b)=(aa^{-1})b=eb=b\) and \(ya=(ba^{-1})a=b(a^{-1}a)=be=b\text{.}\). So such elements \(x\) and \(y\) exist. The fact that they are unique follows from the cancellation laws: if \(ax=b\) and \(ax'=b\) then \(x=x'\) by left cancellation, and if \(ya=b\) and \(y'a=b\) then \(y=y'\) by right cancellation.

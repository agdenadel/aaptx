To prove the fourth statement: Let \(A\) and \(B\) be countable sets. Assume that \(A\) and \(B\) are both countably infinite. Since \(\Z^+\) is countably infinite, we can index the elements of \(A\) and of \(B\) by \(\Z^+\text{,}\), writing \begin{equation*}A=\{a_1,a_2,\ldots\} \text{ and } B=\{b_1,b_2,\ldots\}.\end{equation*}

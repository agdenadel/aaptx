1. \(f\)'s domain, codomain, and range are, respectively, \(\Z^2\text{,}\) \(\R\text{,}\) and \(\Z\text{.}\)

    1. \(f\) is not onto, since, for instance, \(1/2\in \R-\Z\text{.}\)

    2. \(f\) is not 1-1: for instance, \(f(-2,2)=-4=f(2,-2)\text{.}\)

    3. \(f\) is not a bijection since it's not 1-1. (It would be equally valid to answer that it's not a bijection since it's not onto, or that it's not a bijection since it's neither 1-1 nor onto.)

  3. \(f(6,-2)=-12\) and \(f(\Z^-\times \Z^-)=\Z^+\text{.}\)

  4. \begin{align*} f^{\leftarrow}(\{2,3\})\amp =\{(a,b)\in \Z\times \Z\,:\, f(a,b)\in \{2,3\}\}\\ \amp =\{(a,b)\in \Z\times \Z\,:\, ab=2 \mbox{ or } ab=3\}, \end{align*}

    which is the set

    \begin{equation*} \{(1,2),(2,1),(-1,-2),(-2,-1),(1,3),(3,1),(-1,-3),(-3,-1)\}. \end{equation*}

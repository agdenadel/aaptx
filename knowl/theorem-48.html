Theorem7.1.3

(Factorization Theorem) Let \(G\) and \(G'\) be groups, let \(\phi\) be a homomorphism from \(G\) to \(G'\text{,}\) let \(K=\Ker \phi\text{,}\) let \(N\) be a normal subgroup of \(G\) with \(N\subseteq K\text{,}\) and let \(\Psi\) be the canonical epimorphism from \(G\) to \(G/N\text{.}\) Then the map \(\phibar: G/N \to G'\) defined by \(\phibar(aN)=\phi(a)\) is a well defined homomorphism, with \(\phibar \circ \Psi=\phi\text{.}\)

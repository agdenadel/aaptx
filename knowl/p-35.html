Notice that for any sets \(A\) and \(B\text{,}\), \(A\cap B \subseteq A \subseteq A\cup B\) and \(A\cap B \subseteq B \subseteq A\cup B\text{.}\). Also note that if sets \(A_i\) (\(i \in I\)) are mutually disjoint then they are also disjoint, but they may be disjoint without being mutually disjoint. For example, the sets \(\{i, i+1\}\) for \(i\in \Z\) are disjoint but not mutually disjoint. (Do you see why?)

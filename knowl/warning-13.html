Warning3.3.2

Just because a particular map (even an “obvious” one) from group \(G\) to group \(G'\) is not an isomorphism, we do not know that \(G\) and \(G'\) are not isomorphic! For instance, the map \(\phi: \Z\to \Z\) defined by \(\phi(x)=2x\) for all \(x\) is not an isomorphism (since it's not onto), but \(\Z\) is isomorphic to itself, as we will see in Part 1 of Theorem 3.

To prove the first statement: Let \(\sim\) be an equivalence relation on \(S\text{.}\). Clearly, the equivalence classes of \(S\) under \(\sim\) are nonempty sets whose union is \(S\text{.}\). Thus, it suffices to show \(X \cap Y =\emptyset\) for each pair of equivalence classes \(X\neq Y\) of \(S\) under \(\sim\text{.}\). Let \(X,Y\) be equivalence classes of \(S\) under \(\sim\) that are NOT disjoint. Then there exists an element \(z\in X\cap Y\text{.}\). Then by LemmaÂ 7.1.11, \([z]=X\) and \([z]=Y\text{;}\); so \(X=Y\text{.}\). Thus, if \(X\neq Y\text{,}\), then \(X\cap Y =\emptyset\text{.}\).

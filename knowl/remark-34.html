Remark7.1.7

  1. You can think of an equivalence relation on a set \(S\) as being a “weak version” of equality on \(S\text{.}\) Indeed, \(=\) is an equivalence relation on any set \(S\text{,}\) but it also has a very special property that most equivalence relations don't have: namely, no element of \(S\) is related to any other element of \(S\) under \(=\text{.}\)

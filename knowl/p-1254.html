Define \(\Phi:GL(n,\R)\to \R^+\) by \(\Phi(A)=|\det A|\text{.}\). We have that \(\Phi\) is an epimorphism since for all \(A,B\in GL(n,\R)\text{,}\), \begin{equation*} \Phi(AB)=|\det AB|=|\det A \det B|=|\det A||\det B|=\Phi(A)\Phi(B), \end{equation*} and for all \(\lambda \in \R^+\text{,}\), the diagonal matrix having \(\lambda\) in the uppermost left position and 1's elsewhere down the diagonal gets sent to \(\lambda\text{.}\). Since \(\Ker \Phi=H\text{,}\), we have that \(GL(n,\R)/H \simeq \R^+\text{,}\), by the 9.1.6.

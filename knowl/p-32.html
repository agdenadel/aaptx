If \(A\) and \(B\) are sets, then the union of \(A\) and \(B\text{,}\) denoted \(A\cup B\text{,}\) is the set \(A\cup B=\{x: x\in A \text{ or } x\in B\};\) the intersection of \(A\) and \(B\text{,}\) denoted \(A\cap B\text{,}\) is the set \(A\cap B=\{x: x\in A \text{ and } x\in B\};\) and the difference of \(A\) and \(B\text{,}\) denoted \(A-B\) (or \(A\setminus B\)), is the set \(A-B=\{x: x\in A \text{ and } x\not\in B\}.\)

Clearly, \(H\) is a nonempty subset of \(GL(n,\R)\) (for instance, \(I_n\in H\)). Next, let \(A,B\in H\text{.}\). Then \begin{equation*} \det(AB^{-1})=(\det A)(\det (B^{-1}))=(\pm 1)\left(\frac{1}{\pm 1}\right)=\pm 1, \end{equation*} so \(AB^{-1}\in H\text{.}\). Thus, \(H\leq GL(n,\R)\) by the 4.2.5.
